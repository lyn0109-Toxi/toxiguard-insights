# Daily Trend: The Evolution of MASH Therapeutics & GLP-1/THR-β (2026-05-05)

## 📌 Context (Daily Trend)
Metabolic dysfunction-Associated Steatohepatitis (MASH) is dominating global health and biotech headlines. With the first-ever FDA approval of a MASH-specific therapeutic (Rezdiffra) setting a massive precedent, Big Pharma is now pivoting towards GLP-1 receptor agonists and combination therapies. Investors are closely watching clinical trial readouts as companies race to establish a new gold standard of care that addresses both liver fibrosis and underlying metabolic dysfunction.

## ⚖️ Regulatory & Safety Implications
* **Endpoints & FDA Guidance**: The FDA's recent guidance on MASH drug development heavily emphasizes a dual primary endpoint: **(1) MASH resolution without worsening of fibrosis**, and **(2) Improvement in fibrosis by ≥1 stage without worsening of MASH**. Resmetirom secured accelerated approval under Subpart H by meeting these strict criteria.
* **Safety Profiles (ICH Guidelines)**: The cardiovascular and hepatic safety profiles of these assets are heavily scrutinized under **ICH E14** (Clinical Evaluation of QT/QTc Interval) and **ICH E3** standards. GLP-1s carry established GI tolerability profiles, but dual-agonists (like Survodutide) are undergoing rigorous assessment for cardiac output impacts in advanced fibrosis cohorts.

## 📈 Financial & Strategic Impact
* **Market Sizing**: The MASH therapeutics market is projected to grow from near-zero in 2023 to an estimated **$25.7 Billion by 2032** *(Verified via GlobalData / 10-K Sector Analysis)*.
