## 📱 The Power of Relatability Over Credentials
A recent study by **Pew Research Center** found that nearly half of U.S. adults under the age of 50 turn to social media influencers—many of whom are coaches or entrepreneurs with no medical training—for health and wellness advice. 

Despite studies showing that **45% of influencer posts contain inaccurate information**, the public continues to trust them. Why? Because the human brain is wired to prioritize social connection over clinical data.

## 🧠 Why We Trust 'Vibes' Over Science
The human brain reacts faster to emotional connections and visual evidence (the orange side of our graphic) than to complex numerical data (the blue side). 

1.  **Availability Heuristic**: A vibrant influencer sharing their morning routine feels like a more "available" and "real" proof than a 1,000-page clinical trial report.
2.  **Confirmation Bias**: We naturally gravitate toward those who support the lifestyle choices we *want* to make, ignoring the downsides that qualified experts might point out.
3.  **The Algorithmic Loop**: Social media algorithms learn our irrational biases and create an echo chamber of "unverified truths," making it nearly impossible for rational evidence to break through.
