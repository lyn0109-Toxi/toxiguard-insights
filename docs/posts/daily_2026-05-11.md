# The Dawn of AI in Regulatory Science: From Document Screening to QSAR Automation with LY-Type1

![AI Regulatory Science](/Users/leeyoung-nam/.gemini/antigravity/brain/1136e33a-d9b6-4526-9231-15cd55cf8546/ai_regulatory_science_1778505653746.png)

The pharmaceutical industry is currently witnessing a tectonic shift. Regulatory agencies worldwide are no longer just exploring Artificial Intelligence (AI) in drug development; they are officially laying down the groundwork to adopt it for regulatory decision-making.

If you have been following the news in early 2026, the regulatory momentum is undeniable:

*   **FDA & EMA Joint AI Principles (Jan 2026):** The US FDA and European Medicines Agency jointly released the *"Guiding Principles of Good AI Practice in Drug Development"*, emphasizing human-centric design, traceability, and risk-based performance assessments. 
*   **FDA's 7-Step Credibility Framework:** Following their 2025 draft guidance on AI in regulatory decision-making, the FDA requires sponsors to rigorously validate and explain their AI models (XAI) rather than using "black-box" approaches.
*   **South Korea MFDS's Aggressive AI Investment:** The Ministry of Food and Drug Safety has launched its "AI Regulatory Transformation Task Force," investing 22.3 billion KRW to build the **"Pharmaceutical AI Review System."** Their goal is to significantly reduce the new drug review period from the historical average of 420 days. Comparatively, the US FDA already targets around 300 days for standard reviews under PDUFA, and with proper AI integration, these timelines could be reduced even further globally.

We are entering an era where AI agents assist regulatory scientists (Human-in-the-Loop) to quickly parse legacy data, identify risks, and make faster, safer decisions without creating a new bottleneck in algorithm validation.

To explore how machine learning can streamline this workflow, we developed **LY-Type1 (Prototype)**—a conceptual Regulatory Decision-Support AI platform. 

Here is how the LY-Type1 prototype demonstrates the potential of automated ICH-aligned document screening.

---

## 1. Smarter Document Parsing: Breaking the PDF Barrier
One of the largest bottlenecks in regulatory reviews is extracting data from hundreds of pages of scanned legacy documents—specifically, Common Technical Document (CTD) Module 3 (Quality).

Instead of forcing a human reviewer to manually search for specifications and test data, the LY-Type1 platform features an **HALO-compatible OCR & NLP Extractor**. 

![LY-Type1 Dashboard UI](/Users/leeyoung-nam/.gemini/antigravity/brain/1136e33a-d9b6-4526-9231-15cd55cf8546/ly_type1_dashboard_1778505675870.png)

By simply uploading a scanned PDF (e.g., *Pharmaceutical Development reports*), the AI instantly extracts and summarizes the critical sections required for review:
*   **Specifications & Assays:** Automatically identifies impurity limits (e.g., "Not more than 0.1%") and physical properties (e.g., Hardness, Friability).
*   **Bioequivalence:** Captures dissolution profiles, F2 similarity factors, and pharmaceutical equivalence statements.
*   **Stability & Packaging:** Extracts shelf-life and primary packaging materials (e.g., PVC/Aluminium blisters).

![CTD JSON NLP Extraction](/Users/leeyoung-nam/.gemini/antigravity/brain/1136e33a-d9b6-4526-9231-15cd55cf8546/ctd_json_extraction_1778508828782.png)

## 2. Seamless In-Silico QSAR Validation (ICH M7)
Extracting text is only the first step. The true magic happens when the platform connects this data to toxicity prediction engines.

Under the **ICH M7 guideline**, any potential impurities must be assessed for mutagenic potential. LY-Type1 automatically detects chemical entities (API, active metabolites, and excipients) mentioned in the uploaded documents and feeds them directly into our **QSAR (Quantitative Structure-Activity Relationship)** engine.

Without the user needing to manually input SMILES strings, the platform calculates:
*   **Risk Score** (e.g., 0.16)
*   **ICH M7 Predicted Label** (e.g., "Low Risk / Safe" vs "High Risk / Alert")

## 3. Explainability (XAI) for Regulatory Compliance
Following the FDA's "risk-based credibility framework," we cannot simply trust an AI's output without understanding *why* it made that decision. 

![SHAP Explainability XAI](/Users/leeyoung-nam/.gemini/antigravity/brain/1136e33a-d9b6-4526-9231-15cd55cf8546/shap_xai_explainability_1778508805819.png)

To satisfy the stringent requirements for traceability, LY-Type1 utilizes **SHAP (SHapley Additive exPlanations)**. For every QSAR prediction made, the platform provides a feature importance breakdown, showing exactly which molecular substructures (e.g., specific structural alerts or halogen bonds) contributed to the toxicity score. 

## 4. One-Click FDA-Ready Reporting
Finally, the platform synthesizes the extracted CTD data, QSAR scores, and XAI validations into a downloadable, structured PDF report. This closes the loop, allowing a regulatory scientist to review the AI's findings, verify the logic, and include the generated report in their formal regulatory submissions.

---

### Conclusion: Empowering the Reviewer, Not Replacing Them
The goal of prototypes like LY-Type1 is not to replace toxicologists or regulatory reviewers, but to act as a highly specialized **"AI Agent"** that eliminates the friction of data entry and initial screening.

If the industry simply adopts "black-box" AI, it merely shifts the bottleneck from manual document extraction to algorithm validation. Regulators cannot and will not blindly trust automated toxicity calls.

As regulatory bodies officially integrate AI into their pipelines, the key to reducing the 420-day (MFDS) or 300-day (FDA) review cycles lies in Explainable AI. Providing a transparent evidence trail is how we truly accelerate drug development.
