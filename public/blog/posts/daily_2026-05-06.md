## 📌 Context (Daily Trend)
Hypertrophic Cardiomyopathy (HCM) is a significant focus in the cardiovascular space, with limited treatment options for non-obstructive cases. Following the success of cardiac myosin inhibitors in obstructive HCM, the industry has been eagerly awaiting data for non-obstructive symptomatic patients. 

Today, Cytokinetics reported positive topline results from its pivotal Phase 3 ACACIA-HCM trial, positioning aficamten as a potential game-changer for this underserved population. This milestone underscores the shift toward targeted mechanical modulation of cardiac function.

## ⚖️ Regulatory & Safety Implications
- **Functional Improvement**: The ACACIA-HCM trial focused on **exercise performance (pVO2)** and **symptom burden (KCCQ-CSS)**. These endpoints align with FDA’s evolving criteria for functional improvement in rare cardiovascular conditions, emphasizing quality of life alongside mechanical efficacy.
- **Safety Profile (ICH Standards)**: Aficamten’s safety remains consistent with previous studies. Rigorous monitoring of Left Ventricular Ejection Fraction (LVEF) is conducted in accordance with **ICH E14** cardiac safety standards to ensure the potent myosin inhibition does not lead to unintended systolic dysfunction.
- **Path to BLA/sNDA**: With consistent positive data across both obstructive and non-obstructive cohorts, Cytokinetics is optimally positioned for regulatory submissions in early 2027.
